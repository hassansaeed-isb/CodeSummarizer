About the Project

Analyzing the Limitations of AI-based Code Documentation in Capturing Business Logic

This project explores the limitations of AI-based code documentation generators, with a specific focus on their ability to capture domain-specific business logic and contextual knowledge.

Research Motivation

Despite recent advances in AI-based code documentation tools, there's a significant gap in understanding their limitations, particularly when it comes to capturing business logic and domain knowledge.

This research project was designed to quantitatively and qualitatively assess these limitations through:

  • A practical case study involving industrial IoT brake systems
  • Evaluation of CodeT5, a state-of-the-art pre-trained model
  • Comparative analysis of human-written vs. AI-generated documentation

The findings demonstrate that while AI models can successfully document technical aspects of code, they significantly underperform in capturing critical business logic that affects safety, maintenance, and domain-specific understanding.

Project Components
1. Brake Detector Module

A Python implementation of an industrial IoT maintenance log analyzer that detects confusion between "brake" and "break" terms, which has significant safety implications.

2. AI Documentation Simulator

A simulation of how AI models like CodeT5 would document the brake detector code, focusing on pattern-based generation rather than domain understanding.

3. CodeT5 Evaluation

Quantitative evaluation of CodeT5's performance, revealing 87% accuracy on technical details but only 41% accuracy on business logic, 39% on safety implications, and 45% on domain terminology.

4. Interactive Web Interface

This Flask application that provides an interactive demonstration of the research findings for academic presentation.

Fine-Tuning CodeT5

The CodeT5 model was fine-tuned to improve its ability to capture business logic and safety implications in documentation. The fine-tuning process involved:

  • Training on domain-specific datasets.
  • Refining the model to better recognize business logic and domain-specific terminology.
  • Evaluating the model's improvement through qualitative and quantitative analysis.

Key Findings
Technical vs. Business Logic Gap

Our research reveals a 46% performance gap between CodeT5's ability to document technical aspects (87% accuracy) versus business logic (41% accuracy).

Safety Implications

With only 39% accuracy on identifying safety implications, AI documentation tools may pose significant risks when used for safety-critical systems without human oversight.

Domain Terminology

CodeT5 achieves only 45% accuracy in properly using and explaining domain-specific terminology, limiting its usefulness in specialized fields.

Human-AI Collaboration

Our findings suggest that a hybrid approach combining AI-generated technical documentation with human-written business context would be most effective.

Research Paper

This interactive demonstration is based on the research paper:

"Analyzing the Limitations of AI-based Code Documentation in Capturing Business Logic: An Empirical Study with CodeT5"

Abstract: This paper explores the limitations of AI-based code documentation generators, focusing on their inability to capture domain-specific business logic and contextual knowledge. Through a practical case study involving industrial IoT brake systems, we demonstrate how these tools can accurately describe technical aspects of code while missing critical safety implications and domain context. We evaluate CodeT5, a state-of-the-art pre-trained model for code understanding and generation, revealing that while it achieves 87% accuracy in documenting technical details, it significantly underperforms in capturing business logic (41%), safety implications (39%), and domain terminology (45%). Our findings highlight the continued importance of human expertise in creating comprehensive documentation that bridges the gap between code implementation and business requirements.
